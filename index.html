About me

Hello! I'm Sammy Millard (she/her/hers), a post-doc at the Center for Neuroplasticity and Pain (CNAP), Aalborg University, Denmark. I study how we can predict chronic pain development using the brain and the maladaptive neurological and psychological responses to pain that might maintain chronic pain conditions.

Previously, I was a PhD candidate at Neuroscience Research Australia (NeuRA) and the University of New South Wales (UNSW) in Sydney, Australia. My thesis can be found at the UNSW library - ”Unravelling the PAF-Pain Relationship: Modulating Peak Alpha Frequency and Prolonged Pain Sensitivity through Pharmacological and Non-Invasive Brain Stimulation Approaches”.

In my free time I enjoy rock climbing, playing classical guitar, and exploring the outdoors.
